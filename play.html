Your ship

What you're doing out there

Open sea in every direction. Coins drift in deep water; treasure chests sit in the shallows by the islands, worth ten times as much. But gold in your hold is only cargo — it counts for nothing until you've carried it into a harbour and banked it, and if you pile her up on the way home you lose every piece of it.

Work through the contracts, and remember your draught decides everything: where the chests are, and which harbours will even take you.

The controls

The stick is analogue — half a stick is half a rudder. Push it forward as well and the bow thruster shoves her nose the way you're turning; it stops helping above 5 knots. The slider is the engine, and Thrusters overrides it to Stop so you can pivot on the spot. Watch the under-keel reading — speed makes you sit deeper.
Keyboard: ←→ rudder, ↑↓ engine, W thruster, T thrusters, M centre.

The sixteen ships

Every hull is drawn to its true length, beam and draught, and handles accordingly. Turning circle is four ship lengths, so the big ones need over a kilometre and a half to come round.

Questions

Is this ship game free?
Yes. No account, no download, no in-app purchases. It runs in the browser.
Can I play it on my phone?
It's built for touch first — analogue stick for the rudder, notched telegraph for the engine. It plays on desktop too, with arrow keys.
Why can't my ship reach the treasure?
Chests sit in less than 16 metres of water. If she draws more than that she physically cannot get to them, and trying will put her on the bottom. Draught decides where you can sail.
What is squat?
A moving ship sits lower than a stopped one, and the faster she goes the deeper she sits. It's why speed in shallow water puts ships aground. The HUD shows yours live.
Why does the wheel do nothing when I'm barely moving?
A rudder needs water flowing past it. That's what the bow thruster is for, and why the Thrusters button exists — it stops the engines and lets you pivot on the spot.
